## Day 2

Day 2/30 of my deep dive into Voice AI fundamentals


Today I was revising REST APIs & looking at HTTP requests and JSON responses. Strangely, I found myself staring at a metaphor for the deeper life conflicts I've been facing.

In software, when an API undergoes a fundamental change, we create a v2 endpoint. We do this intentionally so we don't break the old v1 clients. 

We give the system time to transition. Human evolution requires the same architecture. Trying to upgrade your life to v2 means you have to clear your cached thoughts and rewrite your logic, but you have to do it without suddenly breaking the connections of the people who still rely on your v1 self.

It takes a shift in how we process errors. 

Maybe growth is just accepting every 404 (Not Found) when you question your path, and countering it with a 101 (Switching Protocols). 

It’s taking a 417 (Expectation Failed) and consciously choosing a 205 (Reset Content) instead of crashing.

It left me with a question I’m still chewing on: When you decide to change, are you a PUT who is completely replacing the old with the new? Or are you a PATCH who is making small, iterative, surgical adjustments to who you are?
